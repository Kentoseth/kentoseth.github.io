Ideas to explore for academic Islamic researchers

Posted on Sat 25 June 2022 in Islamic Studies

Comparative fiqh

Comparing how rulings differ and how they were derived and which Usools were applied for those derivations.

Graphing Islamic scholars and their works

Using graph visualizations to map the works of Islamic scholars and linking their student/teacher relationships to see how works were connected.

Can also be used to compare how a hadith may have been 'weak' in the sihah sittah period but saheeh in the pre-sihah period.

Graphing all notable people in the history of Islam

An extension of the previous idea, this graph visualization could be extended to all notable figures in the history of Islam. Leaders, their governors, saints, scholars and any notable figure.

This graph could be mapped according to significant events + relationships between the nodes. Example: Battle of Badr (a significant war) and the familial relations between the Sahaba(RA) at the time and their Quraysh counterparts.
