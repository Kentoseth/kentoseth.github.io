Intermediate Arabic: Some notes on phrasal structures

Posted on Mon 27 February 2023 in Language

بِسْمِ ٱللَّٰهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ

Ma'rifa (definite)

Types:

  • dhamaair = pronouns
  • 3alam = proper names
  • ishaarah = demonstrative pronouns (haadha)
  • mawsool = alladhee/allatee
  • words that start with 'al'
  • idhaaf = the mudhaaf
  • nidaa = the nakirah (word following 'yaa')

Plural nouns followed by adjectives

3aqil (human)

Broken Plural adjectives have their own irregular forms, like:

  • kabeerun - kibaarun OR sagheerun - sighaarun
  • dhakiyyun - adhkiyaa'u OR ghaniyyun - aghniyaa'u (these do not take tanwin at the end)
  • dha3eefun - dhu3afaa'u OR faqeerun - fuqaraa'u

Ghayr 3aqil (inanimate)

Almost all adjectives are treated as feminine singular (kutubun katheeratun)

Converting noun to adjective with yaa nisbaa

Examples:

Misr - Misriyyun (Egyptian) Jaami3atun - Jaami3iyyatun ('X' university)

Idaafaa constructions

The 'nun' of the dual/plural word (mudhaaf) drops:

(marfoo or mansub/majroor) male / plural: muslimoo or muslimee female / dual: muslimaa or muslimay

Ma3nawiyyah (ismun and ismin)

2 types:

  1. Takhsees = specification like: kitaabu al3arabiyyati
  2. milkiyyah = ownership like: kitaabu attaalibi

Lafdhiyyah (ghayr haqeeqeeyyah) (sifaatun and ismin)

Examples:

taaweelatul qaamati

qaseerul qaamati

Verbs

Negation

lam + fi'l mudhaar (majzum) = did not + present meaning

Which is similar to:

maa + fi'l maadhi (mabni) = did not + present meaning

maa + jumla ismiyyah = emphatic negation (maa is part of kaana & sisters), maa addiraasatu sahlatan (Studying is definitely not easy)

laa + fi'l mudhaar (marfoo) = do not + present meaning

laysa + fi'l mudhaar (af'aal alqalb wa dhanni wa yaqeen) = do not + present meaning

Actions currently in progress negation: maa + fi'l mudhaar (marfoo) = is/are not + present meaning + now

Present tense marfoo

Can indicate 3 meanings:

  1. Azzamanul 7aadhiri = Present tense
  2. haqaa'iqu 3aamatun = universal tools (yaqa3ul maghribu fi shamaali afreeqiyyah - Morocco is located in the north of Africa)
  3. mustaqbal = future tense

Present tense mansub

Can indicate 3 meanings:

  1. ba3da 'lan' linafyi almustaqbal = negate the future (lan as'hara al'laylata ma3a asdiqaaee - I will not stay up the night ...)
  2. ba3da 'an' = ooreedoo an ata3alam al3arabiyyata
  3. ba3da 'li', 'kay', '7attaa' to express purpose = all 3 particles would mean "so that" or "in order to"

Present tense majzum

  1. ba3da 'lam' + fi'l maadhi
  2. ba3da 'li' or 'fal' which are indirect commands = let's do something (linadh'hab ilaa almakkah - Let's go to Makkah)
  3. ba3da 'la' for prohibition = laa takhruj wa7daka (do not leave alone)
  4. ba3da particles of shart = in tasma3 tafham - if you listen (then) you will understand

When a verb has multiple maf3ools

With 2 maf3ools, it can be:

  1. Without mubtadaa/khabr = ahdaytu akhee kitaaban (I gave my brother a book)
  2. With mubtadaa/khabr:
  3. Verbs of certainty = ra'aytu alkitaba mufeedan (I saw the book as useful)
  4. Verbs of uncertainty = 7aseebtu mu7ammadan sadeeqan (I viewed Muhammad as a friend)
  5. Verbs of transformation = 7awwala alkhabbaazu al3ajeena khubzan (The baker transformed the dough to bread)

With 3 maf3ools = khabbaranee akhee (anna) 3aliyan musaafiran (My brother told me Ali is traveling) - anna can be included here to mean the same thing

Majhool (passive verbs)

Forms that cannot have passive:

  • infa3ala (7)
  • if3aw3ala (11)

Intransitive verbs cannot have a passive.

Can be used in the following:

  1. Niyyah (intention)
  2. Where we don't know who did the action = qutila rajulun fi dhuroofin ghaamidhatin (a man was killed in ambiguous circumstances)
  3. Know who subject is but choose to remove it = qutila 3adadun kabeerun min al3iraaqiyyeena (a big number from Iraq was killed)

Difference between majhool & ism maf3ool:

  • majhool acts as verb
  • maf3ool only acts as noun or adjective

Limited conjugation (Frozen) of verbs (fi'lun jaamidun):

Verbs limited to past conjugation:

  • laysa
  • maa-daama
  • ni3ma
  • bi'sa
  • 7abbadhaa

Verbs limited to command conjugation:

  • hab (wahaba)
  • ta3aala
  • halumma
  • haati
  • haaka

Kaana

  1. Past perfect (had done) = Kaana + qad + fi'l maadhi (kuntu qad takharrajtu 3indamaa ta3arraftu 3alayhaa - I had graduated when I met her)
  2. Kaana + fi'l mudhaar (marfoo):
  3. Past progressive (was doing) = kaanat tatanaawalu alfatoor 3indamaa ranna alhaatifu (she was eating the breakfast when the phone rang)
  4. Habit in the past (used to / would do) = kunnaa nal3abu kurratal qadami ba3da almadrasati = we used to play soccer after school
  5. Future in the past (was going to do) = Kaana + mustaqbal (kuntu sa'adrusu fi almaktabati - I was going to study in the library)
  6. Complex/Anterior future (will have done) = sa/sawfa + kaana + qad + fi'l maadhi (satakoonu zameelaatee qad takhrajna ba3da shahrayni - My female classmates will have graduated in 2 months)

Negation of kaana

  1. maa + kaana (had not or did not) = maa kaanat qad darasatil 3arabiyyatamin qablu (she had not studied Arabic before)
  2. lam + yakun (majzum) (had not or did not) = lam takun qad darasatil 3arabiyyata min qablu (she had not studied Arabic before) OR lam yakoonoo yal3aboona ma3an kulla yawmin (they did not used to play together everyday)
  3. lan + yakoona (negate future - will not) = lan takoona ukhti fi albayti ghadan (my sister will not be at home tomorrow)

Other fi'l naaqis:

Kaana + sisters: kaana, sawra, asba7a, laysa, maa, maa-daama, maa-zaala

Kaada + sisters:

  1. muqaaraba (almostness): kaada, awshaka
  2. shuroo3 (beginning): shara3a, tawfiqa = use mudhaar marfoo after these
  3. rajaaoo (wishing): 3asaa, 7araa

Relative clauses

An indefinite noun won't have an explicit relative clause after it (but it will be implied) when followed by a verb = 3arifu taalibatan takharrajati assanatal maadiyah (I know a student WHO graduated the past year) (this is called jumla sifaa)

A dhameer is required if there is a reference back to it after the clause:

haadhaa huwa alkitab alladhee Aqra'uhoo (This is the book which I read (it).)

A relative clause can be followed by a:

  1. shibbi jumla
  2. jumla fi3liyyah or ismiyyah

Nominal sentences

A khabr can come before a mubtadaa if:

  1. The khabr is a shibbu jumla
  2. The mubtadaa is nakiraa (indefinite)

Exception only occurs when mubtadaa is definite for emphasis.

Negation with laysa and kaana

laysatil madeenatu sagheeratan = The city is not small

Maa kaanatil madeenatu sagheeratan / lam takunil madeenatu sagheeratan = The city was not small

Ism faa3il and ism maf3ool

For forms 2-11:

  • faa3il is detected with kasra on the 3ain
  • maf3ool is detected with fat7a on the 3ain

Rare maf3ools:

  • tafa3'3ala (form 5)
  • tafaa3ala (form 6)
  • ifta3ala (form 8)
  • istaf3ala (form 10)

No ism faa3il on if3alla (form 9)

No ism maf3ool on: infa3ala (form 7) and if3aw3ala (form 11)

Ism faa3il can act as:

  • verb
  • noun
  • adjective

Ism maf3ool can act as:

  • noun
  • adjective

7aal

4 types:

  1. ism faa3il or maf3ool in the state of nasb
  2. verbal sentence in the present tense
  3. Nominal or verbal sentence after the 'waw' of 7aal
  4. shibbi jumla after a hidden 'waw' of 7aal

What the 7aal describes has to be definite. If it's indefinite you have to put an adjective after it. (if you don't, it becomes a relative clause)

Nouns

Non-derived

  1. Identified by senses (concrete): sky, flower
  2. Identified by mind (abstract): idea, noble

Derived

  1. Ism faa3il
  2. Ism maf3ool
  3. Ism aalah: miftaa7hun

Masdar

A noun that indicates action/event without time

  1. saree7: regular masdar that we know
  2. meemiyyun (note: a taa marbutaa can be added at the end of these masaadir to increase intensity/exxageration):
  3. maf3alun: sa3aa -> mas3aa
  4. maf3ilun: wa3ada -> maw3idun
  5. mazeed (on the scale of ism maf3ool): aqaama -> muqaamun
  6. sanaa3iyyun: adding yaa (shadda) + taa marbutaa = insaan -> insaaniyyatun
  7. ism masdar: has less letters than usual: a3taw -> 3atawun
  8. marrah: indicates frequency an act happens (an instance)
  9. thulaathee: scale = fa3latun like waqafa -> waqfatun
  10. mazeed: scale = masdar + taa marbutaa (if masdar is already on this scale, add a sifaa like waa7idatun) like ibtisaamun -> ibtisaamatun
  11. ism hay'atee (if masdar is already on this scale, add a sifaa in the idhaafa construction): indicates stance of one doing action when verb occurs: scale = fi3latun like wiqfatun

Ism makaan

Derived noun to indicate where verb occurs.

  1. thulaathee:
  2. maf3alun: maktabun
  3. maf3alatun: maktabatun, madrasatun
  4. maf3ilun: masjidun, marji3un
  5. mazeed: on the scale of ism maf3ool = mustawda3un, mustashfaa

Adding a taa marbutaa indicates abundance of X in that place: mas'adatun (place of many lions).

Context differentiates between ism makaan and masdar meemiyyun

Ism zamaan

Derived noun to indicate time verb occurs.

  1. thulaathee:
  2. maf3alun: mabdaun, mamaatun
  3. maf3ilun: mawsimun, maw3idun
  4. mazeed: on the scale of ism maf3ool = mustahallun, munfata7un
