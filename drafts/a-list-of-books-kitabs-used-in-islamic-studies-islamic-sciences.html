A list of books (kitabs) used in Islamic Studies (Islamic Sciences)

Posted on in Islamic Studies

بِسْمِ ٱللَّٰهِ ٱلرَّحْمَٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ

The following content provides a list of books used in various Islamic Studies (Islamic Sciences) fields. The purpose of this list is to document famous and comprehensive works and not (as other lists do) to compile every possible book within each science.

The reader is urged not to self-study any of these books. It may be argued that Arabic is not an Islamic science, but the complexity of the rules in grammar (nahw), morphology (sarf) and rhetoric (balagha) require a teacher.

Nahw (Grammar/Syntax)

  • Al-Ajurrumiyya by ibn Ajurrum - beginner
  • Nuktatul Irab by Ibn Hisham al-Ansaari - beginner
  • Qatr un Nada by Ibn Hisham al-Ansaari - intermediate
  • Alfiyyah Ibn Malik by Ibn Malik - advanced

Sarf (Morphology)
